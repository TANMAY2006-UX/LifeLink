Frequently Asked Questions

Why should I donate blood?

Donating blood is a noble act that saves lives. A single donation can help up to three people in need, whether they are accident victims, surgery patients, or individuals with chronic illnesses. It's a simple way to make a huge impact in your community.

What are the eligibility criteria for donating blood?

Generally, you must be in good health, be at least 18 years old, and weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs). You should not have any tattoos or piercings done in the last 6 months. Specific medical conditions may affect your eligibility, so it's always best to consult with the donation center staff.

How often can I donate blood?

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). This interval allows your body to fully replenish its red blood cells and iron levels, ensuring you remain healthy.

Is the process of donating blood painful?

You will feel a slight pinch when the needle is inserted, but the discomfort is minimal and lasts only for a moment. The rest of the process is painless. Most donors report feeling fine during and after the donation.

Who can use the LifeLink platform?

The LifeLink platform is primarily for the students and staff of Don Bosco Institute of Technology (DBIT). Our goal is to create a strong, internal network of donors to serve our immediate community.

How is my data kept safe and private?

We take data privacy very seriously. Your personal information is stored securely and is only accessible to authorized NSS volunteers for the purpose of coordinating blood donations. We do not share your data with any third parties without your explicit consent.

I need blood urgently, what is the fastest way to get help?

For urgent needs, use the "Receive Blood" option on our homepage and select "From DBIT Community." This will connect you with available donors on campus for the quickest possible response. You can also contact our team directly through the details on the "Contact Us" page.

What should I do before donating blood?

Before donating, it's important to get a good night's sleep, eat a healthy meal, and drink plenty of fluids. Avoid fatty foods, alcohol, and aspirin before your donation.

What happens to my blood after donation?

After donation, your blood is tested for various infectious diseases to ensure its safety. It is then separated into its components (red cells, plasma, and platelets) and stored until it is needed by a patient.

Can I donate if I have a medical condition?

It depends on the condition. Some conditions like a common cold may require a temporary deferral, while others might permanently disqualify you. It's best to discuss your medical history with the staff at the donation center.

How long does the blood donation process take?

The entire process, including registration, a mini-physical, the donation itself, and post-donation refreshments, usually takes about an hour. The actual blood donation part only takes about 10-15 minutes.

What are the benefits of donating blood?

Besides the immense satisfaction of saving lives, donating blood can also have health benefits for the donor, such as reducing the risk of heart disease and cancer, and getting a free health check-up.

Are there any side effects of donating blood?

Most people feel fine after donating blood. Some may experience temporary dizziness, lightheadedness, or bruising at the needle site. These symptoms are usually mild and go away quickly.

Can I find out who received my blood?

Due to patient confidentiality regulations, you cannot find out the specific identity of the person who received your blood. However, you can be assured that your donation is used to save lives within our community and partner hospitals.

What if I don't know my blood type?

That's perfectly fine! You can still register on our platform. Your blood type will be determined when you donate for the first time. You can then update your profile with this information.

Can I donate blood if I am on medication?

In most cases, yes. However, your eligibility will depend on the type of medication you are taking. It's crucial to inform the medical staff about all medications you are currently on.

What is the age limit for blood donation?

You must be between 18 and 65 years old to donate blood. First-time donors should be under 60.

How can I organize a blood donation drive in my area?

We encourage community-led initiatives! Please get in touch with us through our "Contact Us" page, and our team will guide you through the process of organizing a successful blood donation drive.

Can I donate blood if I have recently traveled?

Travel to certain countries may result in a temporary deferral from donating blood due to the risk of exposure to certain diseases. Please inform the donation center staff about your recent travel history.

What is the difference between whole blood, plasma, and platelet donation?

Whole blood is the most common type of donation. Plasma is the liquid part of the blood and is often used for patients with liver disease or clotting issues. Platelets are cell fragments that help with clotting and are crucial for cancer patients undergoing chemotherapy.
